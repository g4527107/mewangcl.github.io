Prof. Charlie C. L. Wang currently holds a Chair of Smart Manufacturing with the University of Manchester (UoM). Before joining UoM in 2020, he was a Chair of Advanced Manufacturing at Delft University of Technology since 2016 and an Assistant Professor / Associate Professor / Professor of Mechanical and Automation Engineering at the Chinese University of Hong Kong since 2003. He also worked as a visiting professor at University of Southern California during sabbatical leave in 2011. He received his B.Eng. degree (1998) in mechatronics engineering from Huazhong University of Science and Technology and his Ph.D. degree (2002) in mechanical engineering from Hong Kong University of Science and Technology (HKUST). Prof. Wang was the recipient of a few awards, including the ASME CIE Excellence in Research Award (2016), the ASME CIE Young Engineer Award (2009), seven Best Paper Awards and five project-oriented innovative technology awards. He was elected as a Fellow of American Society of Mechanical Engineers (ASME) in 2013, and is currently the Chair of Solid Modeling Association (SMA).

Besides research, Prof. Wang also received a few teaching awards including the CUHK Vice-Chancellor's Exemplary Teaching Award in 2008. His philosophy is that a good teacher not only imparts knowledge but also inspires students to seek truth beyond tradition, definition and image.
